Deconvolution

Deconvolution is the inverse operation of convolution.

$\ast$

$=$

Figure 15: Convolution of an image with a kernel, where $\ast$ denotes the convolution operation.

  • Camera shaking.
  • Diffraction artifacts introduced by the optical system (PSF).

Richardson-Lucy

Let $f$ be the degraded (convolved) image, $g$ the underlying ground truth and $k$ the PSF [Luc74, Ric72].

Bayes' Theorem states:

$$P(g|f) = \frac{P(f|g)P(g)}{P(f)} = \frac{P(f|g)P(g)}{P(g \ast k)}$$

Resulting in the iterative update scheme:

$$g_{i+1} = g_i \left( \frac{f}{g_i \ast k} \ast \bar{k} \right)$$

Variational Models

The goal is to find the underlying image $u$ which convolved with kernel $k$ is most similar to the observation $f$.

Written as functional:

$$E(u) = \|u \ast k - f\|_1 + \|\nabla u\|_{2,1}$$

With $\|\nabla u\|_{2,1}$ as regularization term to suppress noise [CP11].

The goal is to minimize this energy with respect to $u$

$$\min_u \{ \|u \ast k - f\|_1 + \|\nabla u\|_{2,1} \}$$

Convex Problems

Energy Minimization Problem

Primal-Problem $$\min_u \left\{ \|u\|_2\right\}$$
Primal-Dual-Problem $$\min_u \max_{\|p\|_2 \leq 1} \left\{ \langle u, p \rangle \right\}$$

Figure 16: A convex minimization problem.

Figure 17: A saddle point problem.

First-Order Primal-Dual Algorithm

Primal-Problem $$\min_u \{F(K(u)) + G(u)\}$$
Primal-Dual-Problem $$\min_u \max_p \{\langle K(u), p\rangle + G(u) - F^\ast(p)\}$$

Solver [CP11]:

$p_{n+1} = \text{prox}_{\sigma F^\ast}(p_n + \sigma K \bar{u}_n)$

$u_{n+1} = \text{prox}_{\tau G}(u_n + \tau K^\ast p_{n+1})$

$\bar{u}_{n+1} = u_{n+1} + \theta(u_{n+1} - u_n)$

Initialize $p_0, u_0$, arbitrarily, set $\bar{u}_0 = u_0$ and choose step sizes $\sigma, \tau > 0$ and $\theta \in [0, 1]$.

Convex Conjugate

For $f \in conv(\mathcal{V})$ the convex conjugate $f^\ast : \mathcal{V}^\ast \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \cup \{\infty\}$ is defined as:

$$f^\ast (\phi) := \sup_{v \in \mathcal{V}} \{\langle v, \phi \rangle - f(v)\}$$

Proximity Operator

The proximity operator $\text{prox}_{\tau H}(x)$ for a convex functional $H$ and step size $\tau$ is defined as:

$$\text{prox}_{\tau H}(x) := \min_y \left\{ \frac{(y - x)^2}{2\tau} + H(y)\right\}$$

  • Can be difficult to calculate.
  • More robust than the gradient regarding the step size.
  • Can be calculated for non-differentiable objectives.

Adjoint Operator

The adjoint operator $K^\ast : \mathcal{W} \rightarrow \mathcal{V}$ of the linear operator $K: \mathcal{V} \rightarrow \mathcal{W}$ is defined as:

$$(Kx, y)_\mathcal{W} = (x, K^\ast y)_\mathcal{V} \: \forall x \in \mathcal{V}, y \in \mathcal{W}$$

  • $(\nabla u, v) = (u,-\text{div}(v))$
  • $(u \ast k, v) = (u, v \ast \bar{k})$, where $\bar{k}$ is the flipped kernel $k$.

Variational Multiview Deconvolution

Energy Term

$$E(u) = \lambda R(u) + \sum_{j=1}^m T(u \ast k_j, f^j_\delta)$$

  • $f^j_\delta$ the j-th observation of the underlying image $f$.
  • $k_j$ the PSF of the optical system which acquired $f^j_\delta$.
  • $R(\cdot)$ the regularization term with parameter $\lambda$.
  • $T(\cdot, \cdot)$ the cost function.
  • $u$ the reconstructed image.

Regularization Terms

Total Variation [ROF92]:

$$TV(u) := \min_u \{\|\nabla u\|_{2,1}\}$$

  • Piece-wise consistency.
  • Stair-casing artifacts.

Figure 18: A reconstruction result with TV as regularization term plotted as a surface, where the gray-scale intensity is represented by the height. In the bottom right corner the underlying ground truth with smooth intensity transitions.

Regularization Terms

TV-Huber [CP11]:

  • Huber-Norm [Hub73]:

$$\| x\|_{\alpha} := \begin{cases} \frac{\| x\|_2^2}{2\alpha} &, \| x\|_2 \leq \alpha\\ \| x\|_2 - \frac{\alpha}{2} &, \| x\|_2 > \alpha \end{cases}$$

  • $\ell_1$-norm is replaced by the Huber-Norm.

Figure 19: A reconstruction result with TV-Huber as regularization term plotted as a surface, where the gray-scale intensity is represented by the height. In the bottom right corner the underlying ground truth with smooth intensity transitions.

Regularization Terms

Total Generalized Variation [BKL]:

$$\begin{align}TGV^2(u) := \min_{u, w} \{ &\alpha \|\nabla u - w\|_{2,1} +\\ &\beta \|Dw\|_{2,1}\}\end{align}$$
  • Second-order derivative.
  • Smooth transitions.
  • Computationally expensive.

Figure 20: A reconstruction result with TGV as regularization term plotted as a surface, where the gray-scale intensity is represented by the height. In the bottom right corner the underlying ground truth with smooth intensity transitions.

Cost Functions

Squared $\ell_2$-Norm[CP11]:

$$\min_u\left\{\frac{1}{2}\|u \ast k - f\|_2^2\right\}$$

  • Differentiable, therefore widely used.
  • Works well with Gaussian noise.

Cost Functions

$\ell_1$-Norm[CP11]:

$$\min_u\left\{\|u \ast k - f\|_1\right\}$$

  • Known to work well for Poisson and Gaussian noise.

Cost Functions

Kullback-Leibler Divergence[SCJ12]:

$$D_{KL}(f|u\ast k) := \int_\Omega \left( f\cdot log\left(\frac{f}{u \ast k}\right) - f + u \ast k \right) + \delta_{[0,1]}(u \ast k) $$

  • Is only a measure, not a metric.
  • Describes how much information is lost if $u \ast k$ is used to approximate $f$.

Evaluation

Regularization Terms:

  • Total Variation
  • TV-Huber
  • Total Generalized Variation

Cost Functions:

  • Squared $\ell_2$-Norm
  • $\ell_1$-Norm
  • Kullback-Leibler Divergence

Compared to Richardson-Lucy multiview deconvolution [PAS+14] in terms of Peak-Signal to Noise Ratio (PSNR) and runtime.

Summary

  • Variational multiview deconvolution models perform as good as Richardson-Lucy multiview deconvolution in terms of PSNR.
  • For heavily distorted image data the variational multiview deconvolution models generate better reconstruction results.
  • The gain in image quality is paid by a considerably increased execution time.
  • TV-Huber as regularization term and $\ell_1$-norm as cost function should be used in the variational model.

Future Work

  • Merge First-Order Primal-Dual Solver into ImageJ-Ops.
  • Add GPU operations to ImageJ-Ops.
  • Add further regularization terms:
    • Hessian-Schatten Norm
    • Curvelet based
  • Integrate weights for the different views.
